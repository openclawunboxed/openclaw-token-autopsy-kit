# objection handling

eight pushbacks you'll hear, and what to say. the goal is never to convince someone who shouldn't buy. the goal is to remove a real friction so the people who should buy can.

## "we already have logging in helicone / langfuse / whatever"

honest response. those are good tools. the audit is different in two ways.

first, those tools are dashboards. you still have to interpret them. the audit is a written diagnosis from someone who has seen the patterns.

second, those tools don't tell you which lane to change first. the kit ranks the changes and tests whether each one helped.

if they're already paying $200+/month for a logging tool and not getting a clear answer to "what do i do," the audit pays for itself in the first changed lane.

## "we'll do this internally"

honest response. you can. i've seen teams do it well. two things to know.

doing it well takes about 20 to 40 hours of focused work the first time, and the first time has the most landmines. the second time it's faster.

the audit deliverable also includes the kit, so you can run it yourself the next time. you're not buying a service that locks you in. you're buying a faster first pass and the playbook.

if they still want to do it internally, point them at the public-facing version of the kit (if one exists) and offer to be available for a paid hour if they get stuck.

## "the price is too high"

clarify before you discount. ask:

"is the price too high for the outcome i described, or is it too high for what you've been told an audit should cost?"

half the time it's the second. they were comparing against a $50 fiverr cleanup. you're not selling that.

if it's the first, downscope, do not discount. drop a deliverable, drop a workflow, drop the handoff call. keep the price per scope unit honest.

never discount the same scope. it tells the client your initial price was made up.

## "we just need a one-time audit, not a service"

great. that's exactly what the standard offer is. the retainer is optional and pitched separately at 90 days.

never pre-pitch the retainer at the discovery call unless they bring it up.

## "can you guarantee a percentage savings?"

no. and you should not.

honest response: "i can guarantee that you'll have a written ranked list of changes, a before/after baseline so you can prove whether each change worked, and the kit to keep auditing yourself. i can't guarantee the savings number because that depends on which changes you implement and your stack's starting condition. that said, the case study reduction was 44 percent and that wasn't an outlier in the operator audits i've run. i'm comfortable telling you that if your bill is over $500/month, the audit pays for itself in month one most of the time."

if they push for a written guarantee, walk away. that's a client who will sue you for the difference.

## "we don't have time for an audit window right now"

honest response: "the audit takes a week of my time and about 2 hours of yours, mostly the kickoff and handoff calls. if you don't have 2 hours this month, you don't have time to fix the bill. that's fine. when does it open up?"

a real prospect responds with a real date. a non-prospect goes silent.

## "send me a sample report"

never send the full case study report. share an excerpt or a redacted page.

honest response: "here's a 1 page excerpt from a recent engagement (attached). i don't share full reports because they contain client-specific routing decisions. if the format and depth look right to you, the audit produces the same kind of artifact for your stack."

attach a 1 page redacted sample. if you don't have one, build one from the case study before you do outreach.

## "can we just hop on a call and figure out the price?"

no. price is set by scope, and scope is set by the questionnaire.

honest response: "i'd rather give you a price that's honest than a price that's fast. fill out the questionnaire (it takes 10 minutes), then i can quote on the call instead of guessing on it."

if they refuse the questionnaire, that's a no-fit signal. the engagement will have the same chaos the questionnaire is designed to filter out.
