# red flags

the prospects you turn down protect the prospects you accept. ten signals that an engagement will go badly. if you see two or more, decline politely.

## 1. they cannot or will not export run records

no records means no audit. you cannot manufacture data the client doesn't have. if they refuse to set up logging first, they don't want an audit. they want a magic answer.

what to say: "the audit only works on real data. i can recommend a free way to start logging, and we can talk in 30 days once you have a window of records."

## 2. they want to keep the routing changes secret from their team

means there's an internal political fight you're about to walk into. the audit will produce findings that contradict whoever is currently in charge of the stack. if that person doesn't know about the engagement, your report becomes a weapon they didn't agree to.

what to say: "i need at least one technical contact on your team in the loop. otherwise the implementation handoff won't work."

## 3. they want a fixed-percentage savings guarantee

you cannot honestly give one. anyone who does is either lying or pricing the lawsuit risk into the engagement.

what to say: see objection handling, "can you guarantee a percentage savings."

## 4. the budget is below your starter tier and they want full scope

they want the operator audit at the starter price. they will be unhappy regardless of outcome because they entered the engagement feeling underpaid for.

what to say: "for that budget, i can scope this down to {smaller_scope}. or you can wait until you have budget for the full audit. either is fine."

## 5. they answer the questionnaire with "see attached doc" and attach 40 pages

means they don't want to think about their stack clearly enough to answer your questions. the engagement will be a slog of you doing their thinking for them.

what to say: "thanks for the doc. i need the questionnaire answered directly. ten minutes of your time saves us both a much longer call."

## 6. the spend they're worried about is below $500/month

audit doesn't pay for itself at this scale. their problem is real but the right answer is the free version of the kit, not a paid audit.

what to say: "your spend isn't high enough to justify the audit. here's a free starter version of the same playbook. if your bill crosses $500/month, get back in touch."

## 7. they describe their stack with words that don't quite line up

"agents" but they mean prompts. "cron" but they mean heartbeat. "context" but they mean prompts again.

not a deal killer. it does mean the engagement is partly an education project, which means longer hours than you priced for.

what to say: nothing in the discovery. quote one tier higher than the obvious fit, or scope explicitly tighter so the education isn't unbounded.

## 8. they want you to talk to their cto/founder/manager before quoting

if the person you're talking to can't make the buying decision, you're talking to the wrong person. the audit needs an approver from minute one.

what to say: "happy to. let's set up a 30 minute call with {cto/founder} included. once they're in the room we can decide together."

## 9. they push back on the data handling note

real prospects appreciate the redaction protocol. prospects who push back on it usually want you to skip it because they don't want to do the work to prep their logs cleanly.

what to say: "the redactor is non-negotiable. it protects you more than me. if you can't or won't run it, i'll run it on my side and we'll have a one hour delay before audit start."

## 10. they want changes to the sow that move the deposit to the back

means they don't want to pay until they're sure the audit produced what they wanted, which means they're planning to argue about the deliverable. half upfront is the line.

what to say: "the deposit covers the work, not the outcome. if you can't pay 50 percent on signature, the engagement isn't a fit."

## the meta rule

every red flag is a signal that the engagement will eat more time than the price covers. you don't lose money from low close rates. you lose money from bad engagements that take three times longer than scoped. the cleanest "no" you give this month is the most profitable thing you do.
