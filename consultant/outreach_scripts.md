# outreach scripts

three cold outreach formats. use them as starting points, not as copy-paste templates. specificity wins. the more you can reference something concrete from the prospect's public footprint, the higher the reply rate.

## email v1: the curious operator

subject: openclaw bill

hey {first_name},

saw you're running openclaw on {context}. quick question.

most operators i talk to have one workflow eating 30 to 50 percent of their bill and they cannot tell which one. usually it is heartbeat carrying a premium model on routine awareness work, or exact-time jobs riding the main session instead of cron.

i run a small audit that pinpoints the leak in a 7 day window of run records and shows you the exact lane to change first. one operator dropped weekly spend 44 percent in the first pass.

worth a 20 minute call?

{your_name}

## email v2: the consultant referral angle

subject: token autopsy for {company}

hi {first_name},

{referrer_name} mentioned you've been scaling openclaw at {company}. wanted to send a short note.

i do cost audits on openclaw stacks. the pattern that shows up most often is a premium model running every heartbeat tick on awareness work that did not need it. the second most common is a recurring job that wanted cron but lives on heartbeat.

happy to share the deliverable format and a sanitized case study if useful. if it's a fit, the audit takes a week and the report tells you what to change and what to leave alone.

{your_name}

## email v3: the public-signal hook

use when the prospect has posted publicly about an unexpected bill or about scaling agents.

subject: re: your post on openclaw cost

{first_name},

caught your post about {specific_thing_they_said}. that pattern usually traces back to one of three things and almost never to the model price itself.

i run audits on openclaw stacks. one week of run records, a written report, a list of changes ranked by impact, and a before-and-after baseline so you can prove the fix worked.

if you want to sanity-check what you're seeing, i'm happy to spend 20 minutes on a call with you. no pitch, just a read on whether the audit makes sense for your stack right now.

{your_name}

## linkedin dm: short form

hey {first_name}, i saw you're building on openclaw. i run audits that find the lane and routing leaks that quietly pad the bill (heartbeat with premium models, exact-time work on main sessions, etc.). if it's relevant, happy to send a sanitized case study.

## linkedin dm: even shorter

short version: i audit openclaw cost. if your bill stopped feeling cheap, i can usually find the leak in a week. interested in a 20 minute read?

## follow-up rules

- one follow-up after 5 to 7 days, no more
- the follow-up should add a new piece of information, not repeat the original ask
- after two no-replies, move on and revisit in 90 days
- never send a third unsolicited message in a row

## ratios to expect

cold email reply rates are honest signal. expect:

- 5 to 12 percent reply rate on a well-targeted list with specific copy
- 1 to 3 percent on a generic list
- discovery call rate from a reply: roughly 1 in 3
- close rate from a discovery call: 1 in 4 to 1 in 2 depending on fit and timing

if your numbers are below the floor, the issue is almost always targeting, not copy.
