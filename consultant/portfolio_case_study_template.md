# portfolio case study template

every audit you complete should produce a case study you can use in outreach. ask for permission to anonymize at engagement close, not before. clients say yes more often than people expect when the result was good.

fill in the bracketed sections after each engagement.

---

## case study: {anonymized_client_descriptor}

### the operator

{one or two sentences. what kind of business, how they were using openclaw. avoid identifying details unless the client agreed to attribution.}

example: "a five-person ecommerce ops team running one openclaw setup for shared inbox awareness, spreadsheet checks, and twice-daily summaries."

### the symptom

{what made them call. one to three sentences. include numbers if you have them.}

example: "monthly bill jumped from roughly $80 to over $400 in eight weeks. they couldn't tell which workflow was responsible."

### what the audit found

{three to five bullets. the actual structural causes, not generic advice.}

- {finding_1}
- {finding_2}
- {finding_3}
- {finding_4}

### what we changed

{the specific routing or config changes. ranked by impact.}

1. {change_1}
2. {change_2}
3. {change_3}

### the outcome

{the before/after numbers. always cite the verification script output, not your memory.}

- before estimated weekly spend: `${before_amount}`
- after estimated weekly spend: `${after_amount}`
- estimated reduction: `{percent}%`
- quality regression: `{none / one workflow / etc}`

### the bigger win

{the auditability gain or the team capability gain. cost is the headline. visibility is the keeper.}

example: "the team can now answer 'what ran, how often, and why it cost what it cost' in under two minutes. that capability persists past the audit."

---

## consent and attribution

before publishing the case study anywhere, send the client this exact text:

"i'd like to use the audit results as an anonymized case study in my marketing. nothing identifying about your business, your team, or your data would be included. attached is the draft. let me know if you want anything removed or changed before i use it."

attach the filled-in case study. wait for written approval. do not publish until you have it.

if the client wants attribution (some do, especially if the result was a clean win), thank them and update the descriptor to match what they're comfortable with.

## storage

keep one master file: `case_studies/{date}_{anonymous_descriptor}.md`. each case study lives in that folder with the consent record stored alongside.
